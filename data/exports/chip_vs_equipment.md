Over 2021Q1 → 2025Q4: equipment imports +39%, chip imports +27% (both indexed).

Read: if the two indices diverge, China's tool-import and chip-import dependencies are on different paths — the 'self-sufficiency' the debate treats as one number is at least two. Chips run ~2x the dollar value of equipment, so a country can localize tools while staying import-dependent on chips (or vice versa); the trajectories here show which.

Limit: chip imports (HS 8542) are dominated by advanced logic/memory China cannot yet make and by re-export/assembly demand, so the level is not a clean 'consumption' proxy. The TREND comparison is the robust part; a true chip self-sufficiency ratio needs a domestic IC-output series this pipeline does not yet collect.
