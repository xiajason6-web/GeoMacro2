## Red team: the case against

The apparent doubling of domestic WFE share from ~17% to ~37% is largely an artifact of thin, inconsistent measurement rather than a clean structural shift: the ratio is built from a numerator (5-6 listed companies' total revenue) that mixes non-semicap and export revenue and systematically overstates indigenization, a denominator that omits Korea/Taiwan/Singapore imports and systematically overstates the ratio further, and a data series so sparse (domestic revenue exists for only 6 of 13 quarters, all Q1/Q3) and volatile (dropping to 9.3% in 2024Q3 between the two 'doubling' endpoints) that no reliable trend can be extracted; the coincident ~45% collapse in tracked imports (52.4B CNY in 2023Q3 to 28.9B in 2026Q1) suggests the ratio rise may reflect a capex slowdown in tracked import channels as much as genuine domestic capability gains.

**1. The numerator is structurally inflated by two documented 'overstates' biases, so rising domestic revenue may not reflect rising semicap capability at all.**
   Evidence: Methodology explicitly flags that 'total revenue includes non-semicap segments (e.g. Naura's electronic components)' and 'some revenue is export revenue, not China sales,' both marked 'overstates.' Domestic revenue nearly doubled from 10.63B CNY (2023Q3) to 17.36B CNY (2026Q1) using only 6 companies — a base too narrow to separate genuine China-semicap growth from non-core or export revenue growth in those same firms.

**2. The denominator is systematically undercounted, mechanically inflating the ratio across every single quarter in the series.**
   Evidence: Coverage note for every row states imports cover only 'EU27 + Japan + US... KR/TW/SG not yet,' and the bias table explicitly labels this 'ratio somewhat overstated.' Since this gap applies uniformly to all 12 quarters, it cannot explain the change in ratio, but it means every reported ratio (0.169 in 2023Q3 through 0.375 in 2026Q1) is inflated versus a true denominator that would include KR/TW/SG.

**3. The trend is not a smooth acceleration — it is volatile and built on sparse, irregularly collected data, undermining the 'doubling' narrative as a clean trend.**
   Evidence: Domestic revenue/ratio exists for only 6 of 13 listed quarters (2023Q1 partial, 2023Q3, 2024Q1, 2024Q3, 2025Q1, 2025Q3, 2026Q1), all clustered in Q1/Q3. Between the cited endpoints (17% in 2023Q3 to 37.5% in 2026Q1), the ratio actually fell to 9.3% in 2024Q3 (n_companies=5, one fewer than other quarters) before climbing again — a pattern more consistent with noise/company-sample changes than monotonic acceleration.

**4. A sharp, unexplained decline in tracked imports coincides with the ratio's rise, so the 'indigenization' signal could instead reflect an import/capex slowdown in the covered geographies rather than domestic share gains.**
   Evidence: Tracked imports fell from a peak of 58.9B CNY (2024Q3) to 28.9B CNY (2026Q1) — roughly a 51% drop from peak, and down ~45% from the 2023Q3 level of 52.4B CNY. Since ratio = domestic/imports, a shrinking denominator alone raises the ratio even absent any change in domestic capability, and the methodology gives no data to rule this out.

**5. Coverage gaps run in both directions, so the net bias on the reported trend is actually unknown, not just 'understated' or 'overstated' in one direction.**
   Evidence: The bias table shows unlisted domestic makers (SMEE, CETC tools) are 'missing' and this 'understates' domestic revenue, working against the two overstatement biases (non-semicap segments, export revenue) and the import-coverage gap (KR/TW/SG missing). With only 5-6 listed companies as the domestic base and no unlisted-maker data yet collected (flagged as 'Phase 4'), the true direction and magnitude of net bias in the 17%→37% move cannot be determined from the data provided.

**What would change the red team's mind:** A backfilled, complete quarterly series (all 13 quarters, not just alternating Q1/Q3) with: (1) segment-level domestic revenue isolating pure semicap sales from non-semicap and export revenue for the same 5-6 companies; (2) import data extended to include Korea (KOSIS), Taiwan (MOF), and Singapore mirrors to close the denominator gap; and (3) trade-press/tender-based revenue estimates for unlisted makers (SMEE, CETC tools) to correct the understatement bias. If, after these corrections, the ratio still shows a sustained rise from roughly 17% to 37% without the 2024Q3 dip being explained by a company-count artifact, and without the import decline being the primary driver of the ratio's numerator/denominator dynamics, that would substantially validate the acceleration thesis.
