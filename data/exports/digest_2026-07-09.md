## Red team: the case against

The apparent doubling of domestic WFE share from ~17% to ~37% is largely a measurement artifact rather than genuine indigenization: the numerator (domestic revenue) is inflated by non-semicap and export revenue from only 6 listed companies, the denominator (imports) is missing KR/TW/SG entirely and has mechanically collapsed in the most recent quarters, and the entire 'trend' rests on a single-digit number of noisy, unbacked quarterly points rather than a validated time series — so the ratio's rise could reflect import compression and revenue-mix effects as much as any real substitution of domestic tools for foreign ones.

**1. The numerator is systematically overstated because domestic revenue includes non-semicap segments and export sales, both of which inflate the appearance of 'domestic WFE.'**
   Evidence: Methodology explicitly flags 'Total revenue includes non-semicap segments (e.g. Naura's electronic components)' and 'Some revenue is export revenue, not China sales,' both marked as overstating bias, and n_companies is fixed at 6 for every quarter 2023Q1-2026Q1 with no segment-level adjustment applied.

**2. The denominator (imports) has an uncorrected coverage gap that biases the ratio upward, and this gap is not stable — it interacts with a sharp recent import decline to mechanically inflate the ratio in 2026Q1.**
   Evidence: Coverage note confirms imports only cover 'EU27 + Japan + US... KR/TW/SG not yet,' with bias direction 'ratio somewhat overstated.' Imports fell from 50,987,179,469 CNY in 2025Q4 to 28,896,584,948 CNY in 2026Q1 — a ~43% quarter-on-quarter drop — while domestic revenue fell less (22,725,195,848 to 17,358,726,995, ~24%), so the ratio jump to 0.375 is driven substantially by an import collapse in the tracked geographies, not necessarily by faster domestic growth.

**3. The 'doubling' headline compares a single early noisy quarter (2023Q3) to a single late quarter (2026Q1), but the series is non-monotonic and includes a documented dip, undermining a clean 'accelerating' narrative.**
   Evidence: Ratio path: 0.169 (2023Q3) → 0.186 (2023Q4) → 0.153 (2024Q1, a decline) → 0.172 → 0.192 → 0.256 → 0.241 (2025Q1, another decline) → 0.250 → 0.281 → 0.308 → 0.375 (2026Q1). Two quarter-over-quarter declines (2024Q1, 2025Q1) show the trend is not smoothly accelerating, and cherry-picking endpoints obscures this volatility.

**4. The domestic revenue base is narrow and unrepresentative — only 6 listed companies are counted, and unlisted but potentially significant domestic toolmakers are entirely excluded, meaning the 'domestic' figure may already be capturing survivorship/scale bias among large listed firms while missing smaller indigenization activity that the fix (Phase 4 unlisted-maker data) has not yet addressed.**
   Evidence: Methodology states 'Unlisted domestic makers (SMEE, CETC tools) missing' with direction 'understates,' and n_companies remains exactly 6 across all 13 quarters with no expansion, meaning any given quarter's ratio reflects the performance of a fixed small panel rather than the true domestic ecosystem — changes in these 6 firms' revenue mix (e.g., a single large contract) could swing the aggregate ratio without reflecting broader indigenization.

**5. The series itself acknowledges it is preliminary and incomplete, with only one fully clean data point and ongoing backfill/methodology fixes, so treating the 2023Q3-2026Q1 trend as a settled 'accelerating indigenization' finding is premature.**
   Evidence: Methodology explicitly lists 'Only Q1 collected so far — series is one point' as a bias with 'Planned fix: backfill prior quarters,' indicating the underlying data infrastructure was still being built out even as the 13-quarter series was compiled, and none of the four numerator-side or three denominator-side bias fixes (segment revenue extraction, KR/TW/SG mirrors, unlisted maker data) have yet been implemented in the reported ratios.

**What would change the red team's mind:** Segment-level (not total) revenue disclosures for the 6 domestic companies isolating true China-market semicap sales; completed KR/TW/SG import mirror data added to the denominator; inclusion of unlisted domestic makers (SMEE, CETC tools) in the numerator; and a longer, backfilled pre-2023Q3 series with confidence intervals to confirm the trend is monotonic and not an artifact of the 2026Q1 import collapse or narrow company panel — if the ratio still shows a sustained, low-volatility rise from ~17% to ~37% after these fixes, the acceleration thesis would be substantially strengthened.
