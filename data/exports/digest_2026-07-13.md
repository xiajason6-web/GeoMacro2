## Red team: the case against

The apparent doubling of domestic WFE share from ~17% to ~37% is largely an artifact of a mis-specified ratio rather than genuine indigenization acceleration: the numerator (total listed-company revenue) systematically overstates true domestic semicap sales while the denominator (imports) is both structurally undercounted and, in the most recent quarter, collapsed sharply for reasons unrelated to substitution — so the 'share' metric can rise even while absolute domestic capability growth stalls or reverses, and the comparison points chosen (2023Q3 vs 2026Q1) straddle a seasonal pattern that exaggerates the headline move.

**1. The 2026Q1 ratio spike to 0.375 is driven mainly by an import collapse, not domestic strength — domestic revenue itself fell.**
   Evidence: QoQ: imports dropped 50,987,179,469 → 28,896,584,949 (-43.3%) while domestic revenue also fell 22,725,195,848 → 17,358,726,995 (-23.6%). The ratio rose only because the denominator fell faster than the numerator, not because domestic sales grew — domestic revenue is actually lower than the prior quarter.

**2. The import series has a known, undisclosed-magnitude coverage gap (KR/TW/SG missing) that inflates the ratio across the entire series, so the level and its trend are both biased upward.**
   Evidence: Methodology explicitly flags: 'Korea/Taiwan/Singapore exports missing (smaller than EU/JP/US) → ratio somewhat overstated,' and every quarter's import figure is built from only 3 series ('n_import_series=3.0', 'EU27 + Japan + US'), with no correction applied in any of the 13 quarters shown.

**3. The domestic revenue numerator conflates total company revenue with actual China-market semicap sales, inflating the numerator independent of true indigenization.**
   Evidence: Methodology lists two overstating biases: 'Total revenue includes non-semicap segments (e.g. Naura's electronic components)' and 'Some revenue is export revenue, not China sales' — both unfixed as of this data (fix is only 'planned'), meaning the domestic_cny figures (e.g., 17,358,726,995 in 2026Q1) are not cleanly comparable to import figures on either product scope or geography.

**4. Fixed panel of only 6 listed companies for all 13 quarters means the 'domestic share' trend reflects the growth of a small, possibly unrepresentative group, while unlisted makers are excluded in a way that could bias interpretation of composition, not just level.**
   Evidence: n_companies=6 is identical in every single quarter from 2023Q1 through 2026Q1 — no expansion in coverage despite methodology noting 'Unlisted domestic makers (SMEE, CETC tools) missing' (understates), so we cannot distinguish broad-based indigenization from concentration/growth in a handful of large listed firms.

**5. Q1 quarters show a recurring seasonal dip in both series, and the headline comparison (2023Q3 baseline vs 2026Q1 endpoint) mixes different seasonal points, exaggerating the apparent acceleration.**
   Evidence: Ratio dips at every Q1 relative to the preceding Q4: 2023Q4 0.186 → 2024Q1 0.153; 2024Q4 0.256 → 2025Q1 0.240; 2025Q4 0.308 → 2026Q1 0.375 is actually an exception, but domestic revenue itself dips each Q1 (13.03B→9.78B; 18.11B→13.58B; 22.73B→17.36B) and imports dip each Q1 too (57.05B→54.10B; 52.52B→42.89B; 50.99B→28.90B) — the underlying series are not smooth, and anchoring the '17% to 37%' claim on a Q3-to-Q1 span rather than same-quarter-YoY comparisons overstates the smoothness/magnitude of the trend.

**What would change the red team's mind:** Backfilled Korea/Taiwan/Singapore import data to close the denominator coverage gap; segment-level, China-only domestic revenue disclosures (stripping non-semicap and export revenue) for the same 6 companies; inclusion of unlisted domestic makers (SMEE, CETC) in the numerator; and at least 2-3 more quarters of data confirming that the 2026Q1 import collapse is not a one-off (e.g., inventory digestion or order-timing effect) but persists — ideally with same-quarter year-over-year comparisons (Q1-to-Q1, Q3-to-Q3) showing the ratio still rising after these corrections.
